Nanoparticles as Delivery System of Anti-Cancer Medicines

Nanoparticles can be created to encapsulate medicine and deliver it to specific organs in the body. Using nanoparticles increases the medicine's therapeutic index, which is the sweet spot between the amount of medicine necessary to be effective and the amount that causes unnecessary toxicity â€“ because the medicine is directed at the tumor, with the intent of minimizing the effect on healthy cells around it. Nanoparticles also provide high drug loading and controlled release of medicine. Nanoparticles can be designed to be undetectable by the immune system and have surface targeting ligands which direct the particles towards receptors on the surface of tumor cells - the nanoparticles then bind to tumor cells and release the medicine inside them.


Nanoparticles for Photodynamic Therapy

Photodynamic Therapy is a form of phototherapy involving light and a photosensitizing chemical substance, used in conjunction with molecular oxygen to elicit cell death. When used with nanoparticles, PDT can present an effective and selective means of suppressing diseased tisssues without altering the surrounding healthy tissue. It offers a possibility to reach hidden or undetected metastasis. Photosensitizer molecules can be fluorescent molecules and can be used as a photodetection (PD) tool to reveal tumor tissues that cannot be visualized with conventional methods.
